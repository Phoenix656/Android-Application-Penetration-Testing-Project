# Ethics and Responsible Disclosure

## Overview
This document outlines the ethical guidelines followed during the Android application penetration testing assessment.

Ethical conduct is a core requirement of security research and penetration testing.

---

## Authorization
- Testing was performed only on an intentionally vulnerable application.
- The target application is designed for security training and educational purposes.
- No unauthorized testing was conducted.

---

## Legal Compliance
- No production systems or real user data were accessed.
- No laws, regulations, or institutional policies were violated.
- The assessment was conducted strictly within a controlled lab environment.

---

## Responsible Use of Tools
- Security tools were used solely for educational learning.
- No malicious intent or misuse of tools was involved.
- All testing activities were non-destructive.

---

## Data Handling
- No real personal or financial data was used or collected.
- Any test credentials used were dummy values.
- No sensitive information was stored or shared.

---

## Disclosure Policy
- Findings documented in this repository are for academic and learning purposes only.
- No vulnerabilities were disclosed against real-world applications or organizations.
- This repository does not encourage or support unauthorized security testing.

---

## Ethical Statement
The assessment was conducted with integrity, responsibility, and respect for ethical hacking principles. All activities align with accepted standards of professional cybersecurity practice.
